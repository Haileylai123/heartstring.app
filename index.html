Privacy

What Heartstring knows about you.

Last updated: June 19, 2026

This is a privacy policy for an app built by one person (Hi, I'm Hailey). I tried to write it the way I'd want to read it. No legal theater, no dark patterns. If something is unclear, email me: hello@heartstring.app.

The short version

  • Your conversations stay on your phone by default.
  • If you sign in, your progress syncs to a server so you don't lose it.
  • I don't sell your data. I don't show you ads. I don't track you across apps.
  • The microphone is only used when you tap record. It's not always on.
  • You can delete everything anytime. I'll show you how below.

In English

1. What Heartstring is

Heartstring is an iOS and Android app for practicing saying what you actually mean in English. You talk to a cat companion. The app remembers what you shared so you can see your growth over time. After each conversation, the app may suggest a small real-world challenge (like "try telling one friend you missed home this week").

2. What data I collect

Conversations you have in the app. Stored locally on your device. If you create an account, synced to my server (Cloudflare) so you can restore on a new device.

Your account email (optional). Only if you sign in. Used for password reset and Pro subscription. Never shared with anyone.

Subscription status. Stored by Apple/Google when you buy Heartstring Pro. I only see whether you have Pro or not. I never see your credit card.

Crash reports (anonymous). I use Sentry to know if the app crashed. Reports include the error and your device model, not your name or conversations.

3. What I do NOT collect

  • I don't track you across other apps or websites.
  • I don't sell or rent your data, ever.
  • I don't run ads inside Heartstring.
  • I don't read your conversations to train AI models.
  • I don't use third-party analytics that identify you (no Facebook SDK, no Google Analytics for Firebase).

4. Microphone and speech recognition

Heartstring only uses the microphone when you tap the record button. Audio is processed on your device to convert speech to text, then the audio is discarded. The transcribed text is what gets stored, not the audio itself.

If your device uses Apple's speech recognition, Apple may receive the audio for transcription. Apple's privacy policy covers that: apple.com/legal/privacy.

5. Where your data lives

  • On your device (always): conversations, your pet's name, your customizations, your progress.
  • On my server (only if you sign in): a copy of the above, encrypted in transit and at rest. Server hosted on Cloudflare. Located in the US.

6. Who can see your data

Only me (Hailey). I don't have employees. I don't share access with contractors. If I ever need to bring someone in to help, I'll update this page first.

Legal requests: If a government asks for your data, I'll tell you unless legally prohibited. I have not received any such requests to date.

7. How long I keep your data

  • If you don't sign in: Everything stays on your device. When you delete the app, it's gone.
  • If you sign in: Your data stays on the server until you delete your account. After deletion, it's removed from backups within 30 days.

8. Your rights

  • Export: You can see everything in the app. (Settings → Export my data.)
  • Delete: Settings → Delete account. Or uninstall the app if you didn't sign in.
  • Correct: You can edit your memories inside the app.
  • Stop syncing: Settings → Sign out. Your data stays local only.

9. Children's privacy

Heartstring is not for children under 13. I don't knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has signed up, email me and I'll delete the account within 7 days.

10. Changes to this policy

If I change something material, I'll show you a notification in the app before it takes effect. The "Last updated" date at the top tells you when.

11. Contact

Hailey Lai — hello@heartstring.app
